Project 01

Event Window / Event Window Analysis

An event-based dashboard designed to analyze how financial assets behave before and after relevant market events, including geopolitical shocks, energy disruptions and commodity-related episodes.

Event Window Preview

Objective

The objective is to compare asset performance around selected event dates using a consistent event-window framework. The project helps identify patterns in prices, returns, drawdowns and relative performance before and after market shocks.

Methodology

The analysis starts by defining a set of relevant historical event dates and a group of financial assets or indicators. Each asset is aligned around the event date using a common pre-event and post-event window. Price series can be normalized to compare behavior across different periods and assets. The dashboard is intended to support visual comparison of market reactions across events.

  • Define a set of relevant historical event dates.
  • Select a group of financial assets or market indicators.
  • Normalize price series around each event date.
  • Compare pre-event and post-event behavior.
  • Analyze cumulative performance, drawdowns and relative movements.
  • Use the same window length across events to improve comparability.
Project 02

Silver Dashboard / Silver Market Dashboard

A commodity dashboard focused on silver market dynamics, including price behavior, related assets, macro drivers and statistical signals.

Silver Dashboard Preview

Objective

The objective is to monitor silver as a commodity and market asset, combining price analysis, related indicators and quantitative signals to better understand changes in trend, risk and market behavior.

Methodology

The dashboard tracks silver price behavior across different horizons and compares it against related market variables. The project is designed to identify changes in trend, potential breakouts, risk episodes and shifts in market behavior using quantitative indicators.

  • Track silver price behavior across different time horizons.
  • Compare silver against related assets and macro variables.
  • Calculate returns, drawdowns and rolling performance metrics.
  • Identify potential breakouts or changes in market behavior.
  • Use quantitative indicators to support market interpretation.
Project 03

Sectors / Sector Rotation Dashboard

A dashboard focused on sector behavior, relative performance and market rotation across different phases of the investment cycle.

Sectors Preview

Objective

The objective is to analyze how equity sectors behave relative to the broader market, identifying leadership, defensive behavior, rotations and potential regime changes.

Methodology

The analysis compares sector performance against a broad equity benchmark and evaluates relative strength across different windows. The dashboard is intended to support the identification of sector leadership, defensive behavior and market rotation patterns.

  • Track sector performance relative to a broad equity benchmark.
  • Calculate relative returns and rolling performance indicators.
  • Group sectors according to economic or market-cycle characteristics.
  • Compare sector leadership across different time windows.
  • Use quantitative tools to identify possible rotation patterns.
Project 04

Sentiment / Market Sentiment Dashboard

A market-based sentiment dashboard designed to transform financial indicators into interpretable measures of risk appetite, stress and investor behavior.

Sentiment Preview

Objective

The objective is to build sentiment indicators using market data, capturing changes in risk appetite, stress conditions and directional behavior without relying on textual sources.

Methodology

The initial version uses market variables associated with risk appetite, volatility, asset performance and stress conditions. Indicators are standardized and combined to create interpretable measures of market sentiment.

  • Select market variables related to risk appetite, volatility and asset performance.
  • Standardize indicators to make them comparable.
  • Combine individual signals into broader sentiment measures.
  • Monitor changes across time and identify periods of stress or optimism.
  • Use market-based inputs only in the initial version.
